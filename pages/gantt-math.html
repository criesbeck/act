Estimates for when a software project will finish are notorious for being wrong by a factor of two or more. Velocity can provide more realistic, data-based estimates.
What follows is a toy example to demonstrate how Gantt chart type methods predict project completion in the face of delays.
The developers laid out a schedule for coding tasks. They estimated that they should be done by April 22nd.
The developers needed an extra day to finish the first week's work. They look at the Gantt chart to come up with a new project completion date.
Since there was a one-day slippage, they move the completion date one day, to April 25th.
