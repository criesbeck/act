Velocity: the number of story points done in an iteration.
Example A team completes one three-point story and two five-point stories. The velocity for that iteration is 13.
Velocity is the basis for what to expect in an iteration. It can be used in a burnup chart to determine when rescoping is needed.
No partial credit. No story points are earned until the story is complete, tested, accepted by the client, and deployable to users.
Expect erratic velocities for the first few iterations of a new project or a new team. Velocity should stabilize. Whether it increases or not depends on many factors. The team will become more familiar with the project and technologies, but the codebase will become bigger and more complex.
Don't calculate an average velocity. The best predictor of the velocity in the next iteration is the velocity for the current iteration. If things have slowed, there is a reason that probably will persist. This is called the "yesterday's weather" model of predicting velocity.
Don't compare velocities across teams or projects. Story points are relative to the easiest task in a specific backlog.
