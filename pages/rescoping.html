If you can't make your deadline, reduce scope. Avoid
  • letting the deadline slip
  • pushing the developers to work faster
Scope is usually easier to reduce than you think. There are always "must have" features that turn out to really be just "nice to have." There are always simpler ways to provide support a user story.
Eric Ries has said that he doesn't know what your minimum viable product (MVP) is, but it's one-eighth as big as you think it is.
In the book on product development Getting Real from 37signals, you'll find chapters with titles like "Build Less: Underdo your competition," "Less mass: The leaner you are, the easier it is to change," and "Embrace constraints: Let limitations guide you to creative solutions."
In my opinion, the ideal first MVP is a one-button app. Just start the app, and you get your payoff. Think Facebook, Twitter, Flashlight.
