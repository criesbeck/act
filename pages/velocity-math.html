Estimates for when a software project will finish are notorious for being wrong by a factor of two or more. Velocity can provide more realistic, data-based estimates.
What follows is a toy example to demonstrate velocity math. You should first look at how the same situation would be handled by Gantt math.
The developers split the user stories up, until they are what they think are one-day efforts. They end up with 20 stories, each one point. Over previous projects, the developer team's average velocity was 5 points per week, so that's the initial guess. At that velocity, taking weekends into account, the project should finish by April 22nd.
After the first full week of development, the developers finished 4 points of stories. Now they use velocity math to predict when the project might complete, using a burndown chart.
With an actual velocity of 4 points per week, the prediction is obvious. The project will take 5 iterations. It will finish on April 29.
