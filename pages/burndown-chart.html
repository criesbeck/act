A burndown chart displays progress in completing ("burning") story points for the current release.
The primary purpose of the chart is to show at a glance whether the hoped-for release is feasible, or will have to be rescoped.
A burndown chart can mislead. Lack of apparent progress in reducing story points might be because development slowed. But it just as easily could be because scope increased.
The burnup chart uses the same data as the burnup chart, but separates out velocity versus scope.
