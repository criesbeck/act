In at most 4 panels tell a specific story about your app. Two panels for context, persona, and problem, two panels for payoff and wrapup.
Panel 3 is the most important. Start there. Show the screenshot that demonstrates your application at its best. Then work backwards to set up a convincing story. Iterate. Test it on people.
The 4-panel is the acid test of whether you are ready to start. If you can't come up with a realistic example, maybe it doesn't exist. If your story doesn't sell the idea, maybe the idea is not that great. If you can't write a specific example, your developers can't write the code you need.
Sweat the details. They're what make stories work. "Mary, a freshman in Calculus 102, nervous about the midterm in 3 days" not "A student needs a tutor.

Skip the friction. Don't get into logging in, menu selection, and stuff like that.
