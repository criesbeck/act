The task board is a visual display of the state of the current iteration. The board is divided left to right into at least three columns: ready to do, done, and ready for review.
Frequently, related items, such as the burnup chart, are attached to the task board
At the start of the iteration, a card or sticky note for each story that was expanded in the iteration planning session is placed in the ready to do column. A brief summary of the user story is written on the card.
When developers start work on the next story in ready to do, they put their names on the card, and move it into the doing column.
When developers finish a story, i.e., their code passes the acceptance tests and any unit tests created during test-driven development, they move the card into the ready to review column.
