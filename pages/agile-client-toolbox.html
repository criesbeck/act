This is a toolbox for the software product client: the person with a vision of a product, and a team of developers to help build it.
There are many resources for agile developers. They focus on how programmers can radically alter the way they code, to deliver value to clients faster.
There are many resources for agile project managers. They focus on how project managers can help manage risks and remove obstacles in an agile development project.
This resource is for agile clients. It focuses on how to apply agile values and principles to product design, from initial idea formation to a testable product.
The tools in this toolbox are simple practices that agile team have found useful over the past 20 years for delivering value early and often.
Included in the toolbox are some common activities that clients engage in frequently with their developers to make your vision real... and realistic.
Agile is not defined by these tools. You could do all of these things and not be agile. You could do none of them and still be agile. Agile is a mindset that constantly looks for ways in which more value can be sustainably delivered more frequently.
Agile development is a contact sport. Being an agile client requires frequent, effective communication with developers, not about code or technology, but about end user product value.
Here are some starting points for those just beginning the agile journey:

  • Planning the next slice
  • Estimating and tracking
