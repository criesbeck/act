Use this to sketch a top-level view of your primary user types and their primary user stories.
Example: A marketplace app has buyers and sellers. A buyer has stories such as browsing, comparing, ordering, saving profile information, and so on. A seller has stories about managing a catalog of offerings, tracking views, order, and accounts, and so on.
Assume there are more types of users than you first think. For example, buyers and sellers aren't the only users that interact with a marketplace app. There are sysadmins, who have stories about real-time monitoring of system traffic and server load, managing and backing up data, and so on. There are those in the organization tasked with getting data about what's selling, when, to whom. There are payment services that have to interact with the back-end of your application.
